A 4-step template for clear, academic speaking

In academic speaking, a good answer is not just long — it is structured. The O-R-E-S template gives you four steps to build a complete, logical response. Use it whenever you need to explain your opinion in a discussion, interview, or speaking exam.
The Four Steps
O Opinion
State what you believe — clearly and directly.
"I believe that..."
"In my view..."
"I am of the opinion that..."
R Reason
Explain why you hold this opinion.
"This is because..."
"The main reason is..."
"One key factor is..."
E Example
Give a real situation that supports your reason.
"For instance..."
"In my own experience..."
"A clear example is..."
S Summary
Close with one sentence that restates your position.
"Therefore..."
"That is why I think..."
"For these reasons..."
Model Response
QUESTION Do you think online learning is better than classroom learning?
OIn my view, online learning has real advantages for motivated students.
RThe main reason is that it allows learners to study at their own pace and revisit difficult content.
EFor instance, when I struggled with a grammar point, I was able to rewatch the explanation three times — something impossible in a regular classroom.
SThat is why I believe online learning can be highly effective, provided the student is self-disciplined.

How to use this sheet

  • Keep this open during every class — you will use it in every speaking session.
  • Do not read phrases word-for-word. Pick one from each step that feels natural for you.
  • A good O-R-E-S response takes about 40–60 seconds. Do not try to make it longer than that.
  • If you forget a step during a discussion, just pause and say "Let me add one example..." — it sounds natural, not like a mistake.
