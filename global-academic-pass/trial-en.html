About This Course

What Is This Course?

Building on TOMODACHI's communicative English foundation, this special course develops logical and academic English expression. Using IELTS frameworks, students learn to structure their opinions clearly and engage in deep discussions with international partners.

From "I can speak English" to "I can express my ideas in English". We are exploring this as a full course designed for students preparing for university entrance exams, study abroad, or overseas higher education — and this is a 4-session trial to shape what it becomes.

  Your feedback after each session is invaluable. This course is built by your voice.

Logical Structure

The O-R-E-S framework trains you to deliver opinions, reasons, examples, and summaries in a coherent flow.

Academic Vocabulary

Upgrade from everyday English to IELTS-level expression. Word choice transforms how you come across as a speaker.

Cross-cultural Discussion

Debate real-world topics with international peers and develop the dialogue skills the world expects.

Writing Reinforced

Apply the same logical structure to IELTS Task 2 writing and sharpen your academic writing skills.

  Logic Template: O-R-E-S

IELTS Speaking Part 3 demands the ability to develop an opinion. These 4 steps let you answer any question with structure and confidence.

O

Opinion

I believe that... / In my view...

R

Reason

This is because... / The main reason is...

E

Example

For instance... / In my country, for example...

S

Summary

Therefore... / That is why I think...

  Model Answer Using O-R-E-S

Question: "Do you think online learning is better than classroom learning?"

O In my view, online learning has real advantages for motivated students.
R The main reason is that it allows learners to study at their own pace and revisit difficult content.
E For instance, when I struggled with a grammar point, I was able to rewatch the explanation three times — something impossible in a regular classroom.
S That is why I believe online learning can be highly effective, provided the student is self-disciplined.
  Academic Vocabulary: Upgrade Your Words

Swapping everyday words for more precise, academic alternatives can dramatically change how you come across as a speaker.

Instead of "good"
Beneficial
"It is beneficial because..."
Instead of "bad"
Harmful
"This could be harmful to..."
Instead of "good"
Essential
"It is essential for students to..."
Instead of "bad"
Challenging
"One challenging aspect is..."
Instead of "I think":   "I am of the opinion that..."  /  "I would argue that..."
  1-on-1 Exchange — Session 1

A 15-minute paired conversation activity where students put the O-R-E-S structure into real practice.

Discussion Topic

"Is online learning better than classroom learning?"

1

Person A shares their opinion

Develop and deliver a response using the O-R-E-S structure

2

Person B asks a follow-up question

"How does it differ in your country?" / "Can you give an example?" / "What's the downside of that view?"

3

Switch roles — Person B shares their opinion

Also using the O-R-E-S structure

4

Open discussion → Mutual feedback

Close by sharing specific expressions or ideas you appreciated from your partner
